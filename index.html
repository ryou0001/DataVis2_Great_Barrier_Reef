Tensions of the Great Barrier Reef Region

Residents' concerns for the preservation of the Great Barrier Reef exist in tension with the economic realities of the area.

The Great Barrier Reef Region describes an area that includes the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park and six Natural Resource Management (NRM) Regions. Residents of the Great Barrier Reef (GBR) live in these regions.

A survey conducted in 2017 sought to find out residents' feelings both about the Great Barrier Reef, and threats to the Reef.



Residents tended to speak positively of the Great Barrier Reef, with the overwhelming majority strongly agreeing with the statement "I love that I live beside the GBR".

More than 94% of residents had visited the Great Barrier Reef before, and more than 87% had visited at least once in the previous twelve months.



When residents were asked to list the first words that come to mind when they think of the Great Barrier Reef, the most common words were positive adjectives such as beautiful and beauty, as well as nouns associated with the reef such as coral and fish.
However, it was also common for residents to report more negative words and phrases such as bleaching and endangered. Many residents also gave the words tourism and tourist.




Jobs in the Great Barrier Reef region tend to follow a similar pattern to jobs in Australia. No one job type goes beyond 12% of the region's jobs. Australia's top 3 most common job types, Health care and social assistance, Retail trade, and Education and training all appear as top 5 most common job types for the region. Accommodation and food services and Construction also appear in Australia's top 10 most common job types.

However, when two less common job types are examined - mining and tourism - the tensions of the region become clear.



Mining and Tourism

Mining and tourism are both significant contributors to jobs in the Great Barrier Reef region.
